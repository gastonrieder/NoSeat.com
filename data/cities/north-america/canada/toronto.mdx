## Getting to know the network
Toronto's public transport system is operated by the Toronto Transit Commission (TTC), serving Canada's largest city with an extensive network of subways, streetcars, and buses. The GO Transit network provides regional connections across the Greater Toronto Area (GTA).

### PRESTO Card
The **PRESTO card** is {frontmatter.metaTitle}'s reusable smart card:

- Buy it at any subway station, Shoppers Drug Mart store, or GO Transit station. A PRESTO card costs $6.
- Load funds for PAYG fares or add passes onto it
- Special discounts available for students, seniors, and youth
- Valid across the entire Greater Toronto Area including GO Transit

### Credit/Debit Cards
Credit and debit card payments are accepted on:
- UP Express (airport train)
- Some GO Transit services
- Coming soon to TTC services

### Which payment method should I choose?

For visitors, the **PRESTO card** is the recommended option because:
- It works across all transport services
- Offers transfer discounts between TTC services
- Can be used for both local TTC and regional GO Transit trips
- Easier than handling cash
- Can be reloaded at any station or via the PRESTO app

## Airports

### Toronto Pearson International Airport (YYZ)
Two main public transport options:
- UP Express: Premium airport train service to downtown Toronto (Union Station)
  - 25-minute journey
  - Accepts PRESTO, credit cards, or mobile payments
- TTC Airport Rocket Bus (192)
  - Connects to Kipling Station on Line 2
  - Regular TTC fare with PRESTO card

### Billy Bishop Toronto City Airport (YTZ)
- Free shuttle bus to Union Station
- Short walk to streetcar routes
- Ferry service (included in flight ticket)

## Free and discounted travel
- Children 12 and under ride free on TTC
- Senior and student discounts available with PRESTO
- Family pass available on weekends and holidays
Visit the [TTC website](https://ttc.ca) for more details.
