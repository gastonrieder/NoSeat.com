## Getting to know the network
New York City's public transport system is one of the largest and most extensive in the world.
The subway runs 24/7 and together with the iconic yellow cabs, they're a symbol of the city that never sleeps.
Getting around on public transport is not just convenient, it's the best way to experience the Big Apple.

## How to pay

- Your contactless bank card
- OMNY Card
- MetroCard (being phased out)

### Contactless bank card
Your **contactless bank card** is your ticket! You pay by tapping on any OMNY reader. You can also pay via Apple Pay or Google Pay on your smartphone or smartwatch. Remember to always use the same device when tapping.

Your fare will be automatically calculated and deducted. There are no daily or weekly caps yet, but they are coming soon.

**Tips**: make sure your bank card is enabled for contactless payments and overseas transactions. Also make sure there are no hidden currency conversions fees.
For general guidance on using your contactless bank card as a payment method, see our [contactless bank card guide](URL)

### OMNY Card
The **OMNY card** is {frontmatter.metaTitle}'s reusable smart card:

- Buy it at retail locations across the city including many convenience stores and pharmacies
- Top it up for PAYG fares
- No registration required
- Perfect if you don't have a contactless bank card or prefer to use cash

### MetroCard
The **MetroCard** is being phased out but still works:

- Buy it at any subway station
- Top it up for PAYG fares or load unlimited ride passes onto it
- Will be completely replaced by OMNY by 2024

## Airports

### JFK
Take the AirTrain (separate fare) to connect with:
- Subway: A, E lines at Howard Beach or Jamaica stations
- LIRR: Jamaica Station to Penn Station
### LaGuardia (LGA)
- Take the Q70 SBS bus (free) to connect with subway lines
- M60 SBS bus to Manhattan (regular fare)

### Newark (EWR)
- Take AirTrain Newark (separate fare) to connect with NJ Transit trains to Penn Station
- Express buses also available
