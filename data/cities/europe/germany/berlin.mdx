## Getting to know the network
Berlin's public transport system is a marvel of German efficiency. 
The city's integrated network of U-Bahn, S-Bahn, trams, and buses makes it easy to explore everything from historic landmarks to trendy neighborhoods.
Getting around on public transport is the smartest way to experience the German capital.

### Ticket Types and Prices
- Single Ticket (Einzelfahrschein): €3.00
- Day Pass (Tageskarte): €8.80
- 7-Day Pass (7-Tage-Karte): €34.00
- Group Day Ticket (Kleingruppen-Tageskarte): €25.50

### Where to Buy
- BVG ticket machines at stations
- BVG app
- Tourist information centers
- Selected shops and kiosks

### Berlin WelcomeCard
- Includes unlimited public transport
- Free entry to many museums
- Discounts on attractions
- Available for 2-6 days

## Zones
- Zone A: Central Berlin
- Zone B: Up to city limits
- Zone C: Surrounding areas (including Potsdam)

## Airports

### Berlin Brandenburg Airport (BER)
- **FEX (Airport Express)**:
  - Direct service to Berlin Central Station
  - Journey time: 30 minutes
  - Runs every 30 minutes
  - Regular tickets valid

- **S-Bahn (S9, S45)**:
  - More stops but cheaper
  - Journey time: 50 minutes
  - Regular tickets valid
  - Runs frequently

### Operating Hours
- U-Bahn & S-Bahn: 4:30 AM - 1:00 AM
- 24-hour service on weekends
- Night buses replace regular routes overnight

### Best routes for sightseeing

#### **Bus 100**
- **Route**: Alexanderplatz ↔ Zoologischer Garten
- **Highlights**:
  - Brandenburg Gate
  - Reichstag
  - Victory Column
  - Kaiser Wilhelm Memorial Church

#### **Tram M1**
- **Route**: Prenzlauer Berg ↔ Rosenthal
- **Highlights**:
  - Hackescher Markt
  - Kastanienallee
  - Mauerpark
  - Pankow

#### **U2 Line**
- **Route**: Pankow ↔ Ruhleben
- **Highlights**:
  - Alexanderplatz
  - Potsdamer Platz
  - KaDeWe
  - Olympic Stadium

#### **S-Bahn Ring**
- **Route**: Circular route around central Berlin
- **Highlights**:
  - East Side Gallery
  - Tempelhof Field
  - Treptower Park
  - Charlottenburg Palace
